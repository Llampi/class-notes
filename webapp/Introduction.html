Let us study how different taxes affect the budget set. We will study two types of taxes: sale tax and income tax. These taxes affect different parts of the budget line and, consequently, the budget set.

The sale tax is a per-unit tax imposed on the consumption of either good 1 or good 2. For example, if a per-unit tax of t dollars is imposed on good 1, then the consumer pays

t + pi

per unit of good 1 that she acquires. After the tax the budget set is

(p 1 + t) x 1 + p 2 x 2 ≤ m.

The next picture illustrates the budget restriction after the sale tax.

The income tax is an amount of money that the consumer has to give to the government. For example, if an income tax T is introduced, the effective income of the person reduces to

m-T

After the tax the budget set is

p 1 x 1 + p 1 x 2 ≤ m - T.
